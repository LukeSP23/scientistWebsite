Scientist Name

Stephen Hawking, born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England, was a brilliant physicist known for his groundbreaking work on black holes and cosmology. Despite being diagnosed with ALS at 21 and given only a few years to live, Hawking defied the odds, earning his PhD and becoming a world-renowned scientist. He developed the theory of Hawking Radiation, proving black holes emit radiation, and authored the best-selling book A Brief History of Time, which made complex scientific concepts accessible to the public. Confined to a wheelchair and reliant on a voice synthesizer, Hawking continued to inspire through his resilience, popular science writings, and appearances in media. He received numerous honors, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and dreamed of traveling to space, symbolizing his lifelong curiosity and determination.
